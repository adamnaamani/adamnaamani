> "_Born from an idea of three-dimensional living, Fifteen Fifteen introduces horizontality to an urban tower._" _– Bosa Properties_

**Overview**

- Developed by Bosa Properties
- 202 units
- 42 stories
- Units starting at $900,000
- Estimated completion 2027

**Precision-driven architecture**

Coming soon to the coveted Coal Harbour neighbourhood is a visually striking development offering a new paradigm of living. Fifteen Fifteen is located just across the street from another up-and-coming architectural masterpiece, [Alberni by Kengo Kuma](https://adamnaamani.com/alberni-by-kuma/). Designed by globally renowned architect, Ole Scheeren, Fifteen Fifteen is characterized by glass-enclosed cantilevers reaching out towards the Harbour, North Shore mountains, and city, drawing the natural setting into the privacy of personal space.

**Studio, One, Two and Three Bedroom Residences**

Ole Scheeren approached the interiors of the residences with the same intelligence and rigour that define the architecture and engineering of this singular building. With close to 70 completely unique floorplans, the architect considered every element of the living experience, meticulously crafting the experience to maximize space and light while maintaining privacy and security.

Studio and One Bedroom homes are highly functional and efficient. They combine the flexibility of modern, open‐plan living with privacy and seclusion. They were developed with a clear zoning that creates a sense of openness and generosity with intelligent solutions for daily living.Fifteen Fifteen is situated along West Georgia Street and Cardero Street, within walking distance of all the best amenities Downtown Vancouver has to offer. Some of my favourite restaurants are nearby such as [Cardero's](https://www.vancouverdine.com/carderos/), [Marutama Ra-men](https://marutama.ca/), and [Joe Fortes](https://www.joefortes.ca/) plus many others along Robson Street. There is also convenient public transit access, with Granville Skytrain Station only 1.4km away and bus stops along West Georgia Street.
