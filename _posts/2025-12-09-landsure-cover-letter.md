Landsure,

I've been in the software product space since 2012; as a technical founder of a real estate portal leading a cross-functional team, full-stack engineer with a transaction management startup, and full-stack engineer for one of the leading Canadian suiting retailers. I studied Business in Real Estate, Urban Land Economics, and Real Estate Trading Services at Sauder School of Business, as well as Business Administration at Douglas College.

My experience in technology-driven companies has given me an extensive understanding of agile development frameworks, using platforms like Pivotal Tracker, Jira, Basecamp, Trello and Slack. This includes pair programming, weekly sprints, daily standup, and retrospectives. I veer more towards the ShapeUp method by scoping projects to 4-6 week cycles, asynchronous communication, small agile teams, and managers of one.

As a real estate practitioner, I've always incorporated technology by building it myself, or experimenting with bleeding edge software to improve business operations. I've spent inordinate amounts of time at the intersection of SaaS and regulated environments, founding the first platform in Canada to combine OpenData and MLS® feeds, working with stakeholders to ensure compliance.

Currently, I am with the largest cloud-based brokerage eXp Realty as a real estate advisor, therefore I have hands-on experience with real estate conveyancing, products like Autoprop, LOTR, and ParcelMap. To address the elephant in the room, why would I be applying for a technical product manager role instead of starring in Selling Vancouver on Netflix? It is what I am passionate about. It is what I know best, and I have a desire to contribute to building better tools for real estate professionals, which is ultimately in the public interest.

Best regards,
Adam Naamani
